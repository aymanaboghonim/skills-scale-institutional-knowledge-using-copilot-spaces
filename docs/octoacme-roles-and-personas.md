# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests (unit, integration) and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Participate actively in sprint ceremonies (planning, standups, retros)
- Maintain code quality and follow team standards

### Key Accountabilities
- **Code Quality**: Ensure code is tested, reviewed, and maintainable
- **Delivery Commitment**: Meet sprint commitments or raise blockers early
- **Collaboration**: Review peers' code promptly (within 24 hours)
- **Technical Excellence**: Follow best practices, refactor when needed
- **Documentation**: Keep technical docs and comments current
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Help onboard new developers, share learnings

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed
- Slack/async updates on progress and blockers
- Direct communication with PM/PdM on scope questions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Managers**: Clarify requirements, provide technical feasibility input, suggest alternatives
- **With Project Managers**: Report progress, raise blockers, estimate effort
- **With QA/Testing**: Collaborate on test strategy, fix bugs, improve testability
- **With Other Developers**: Code reviews, pair programming, knowledge sharing

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog based on value and impact
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research, data analysis, and metrics
- Write clear user stories with acceptance criteria
- Make go/no-go decisions on features

### Key Accountabilities
- **Customer Value**: Ensure features deliver measurable customer and business value
- **Prioritization**: Make clear, defensible prioritization decisions
- **Stakeholder Alignment**: Keep stakeholders informed and aligned on roadmap
- **Success Metrics**: Define and track metrics that prove impact
- **Market Awareness**: Stay informed on customer needs, competition, market trends
- **Backlog Health**: Maintain a groomed, prioritized backlog ready for development

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs
- Sprint demos and feature showcases
- User feedback sessions and research readouts

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Project Managers**: Align on priorities, timelines, and resource needs; escalate scope changes
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements, discuss technical constraints, review solutions
- **With Stakeholders**: Present roadmap, gather feedback, manage expectations
- **With Customers**: Conduct research, validate solutions, gather feedback

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication
- Track progress and identify blockers
- Escalate issues appropriately and timely

### Key Accountabilities
- **Delivery**: Ensure projects complete on time and within scope
- **Risk Management**: Identify and mitigate risks before they become issues
- **Communication**: Keep all stakeholders informed with accurate, timely updates
- **Process**: Facilitate effective ceremonies and maintain team workflows
- **Transparency**: Maintain visible project status and clear accountability
- **Unblocking**: Remove impediments and enable team productivity

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation
- Daily check-ins with team on blockers
- Regular syncs with Product Manager and Tech Lead

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Managers**: Align on priorities, discuss scope and timeline trade-offs
- **With Developers**: Understand progress, identify blockers, facilitate solutions
- **With Stakeholders**: Provide status updates, manage expectations, gather requirements
- **With Other PMs**: Coordinate dependencies, share resources, align timelines

## QA / Testing Professionals

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals ensure quality throughout the development lifecycle by designing test strategies, executing tests, and advocating for quality practices. They validate that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans and test cases
- Perform manual and automated testing
- Validate features against acceptance criteria
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Collaborate with developers on testability and bug fixes
- Maintain test automation frameworks and suites
- Advocate for quality practices across the team

### Key Accountabilities
- **Quality Validation**: Ensure features meet quality standards before release
- **Test Coverage**: Maintain comprehensive test coverage for critical paths
- **Bug Advocacy**: Clearly document and prioritize defects
- **Testing Efficiency**: Balance manual and automated testing appropriately
- **Early Involvement**: Participate in planning to influence testability
- **Release Confidence**: Provide clear go/no-go recommendations

### Goals
- Prevent bugs from reaching production
- Reduce time spent on manual regression testing through automation
- Improve overall product quality and user experience
- Enable faster, more confident releases

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Bug reports and defect tracking
- QA sign-off on features
- Quality metrics and test coverage reports
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Review acceptance criteria early, collaborate on testability, verify bug fixes
- **With Product Managers**: Understand feature intent, clarify edge cases, validate user flows
- **With Project Managers**: Report testing progress, highlight quality risks, estimate test effort
- **With Stakeholders**: Communicate quality status, demo tested features
